Classification Of Pollutants

A Model which detects and predicts the pollutants present in the water body and displays it!!

Water bodies are essential resources for life on earth, supporting a wide range of aquatic ecosystems and providing a vital source of freshwater for human use. However, these bodies of water are vulnerable to pollution from a variety of sources, including industrial activities, agricultural runoff, and household waste. These pollutants can include heavy metals, plastics, pesticides, and other harmful chemicals.

Metals such as mercury, lead, and cadmium are toxic to aquatic life and can accumulate in the food chain, posing a risk to human health. Plastics are another major pollutant of water bodies, causing harm to marine life through ingestion and entanglement.

Other pollutants, such as nutrients from agricultural runoff, can lead to the overgrowth of algae and other aquatic plants, causing harmful algal blooms that can be toxic to both aquatic life and humans. The exploitation of water bodies by these pollutants is a significant environmental and public health concern. It highlights the need for better management practices and regulations to minimize pollution and protect these vital resources for future generations.
